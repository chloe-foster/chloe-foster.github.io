Client Information
Client's name and final decision maker (if different) Phoenix Force Robotics, Nikhil Joshi
Client's business (website) name Phoenix Force Robotics
Redesign, or brand new website? Redesign
Type Identity/informational
Client's goals Internet presence comparable to other FIRST FRC teams, as well as an easy way for parents of team members to view information (competition stream links, schedule updates, robots from past years, etc.)
Audience Information
Audience(s) Issues, Concerns, and Needs Approach

Team Members

High schoolers that are part of Phoenix Force Robotics.

  • High schoolers already associated with the team
  • Is generally proficient with computers, although not necessarily (especially outside of the Software subteam)
  • Will be accessing from variety of platforms (primarily iOS for mobile and Windows for desktop)
  • Internet access is generally high bandwidth while on campus (10+MBps); wide variety while off campus but generally skewed high
  • Resolutions will likely be 1080p+ for mobile; primarily 768p for desktop (school laptop resolution)
  • Primarily using Chrome (preinstalled on school computers) and Safari (iOS default)
  • Need easy way to access team information to present to non-team-members
  • Want minimal fluff/filler/extra steps to access needed info
  • Needs to show team sponsors to satisfy the Business subteam
  • Needs to be easy to update so that the Business subteam can do so themselves without taking people away from the Programming subteam
  • Website should be laid out in a way that necessitates minimal digging for relevant pages
  • Website should be very easy on the eyes; no pointless reactive design that just makes it infuriating to look through
  • Website SHOULD NOT have season-relevant information that isn't available via Slack already
  • Website should have team sponsors as an easily-findable page accessible from the portal
  • Website should have one of the following - A) an easy way for them to add content on the fly (via some sort of login system), B) an included script that converts text documents into webpages that can easily be uploaded to the website server, thus saving them from manually writing HTML

Team Parents

Parents of Phoenix Force Robotics members.

  • Parents associated with the team by proxy
  • Wide range of potential computer proficiency
  • Will be accessing from variety of platforms (can't be properly predicted)
  • Likely quality internet connections
  • Resolutions will likely be 1080p+ for both desktop and mobile
  • Primarily using Chrome (most used web browser)
  • Need easy way to access team-relevant such as calendar for meals and competitions
  • Need one place to look for information about upcoming team events (competition itineraries and Twitch stream links, celebrations [first cut, robot reveal, etc.], etc.)
  • Needs to work towards maximizing odds that parents have accurate information
  • Should be easy for parents to use site to show off what their child has been doing to other parents
  • Website should have minimum bells and whistles, and be as consise as possible
  • Website should have a dedicated 'for parents' portal that brings them to any relevant information
  • Website needs information redundancy with other parent-targeted communication vectors (email and word-of-mouth from student) to maximize the odds that all parents know everything they need to
  • Robot pages should have a background section explaining fundamentals of what the robot needed to do

Other FRC team members

High schoolers that are NOT part of Phoenix Force Robotics, but are part of another FIRST FRC team.

  • High schoolers not associated with Phoenix Force Robotics but are associated with FIRST FRC
  • Is generally proficient with computers, although not necessarily (especially outside of their Software subteam equivalent)
  • Will be accessing from variety of platforms
  • Likely high internet bandwidth available
  • Resolutions will likely be 1080p+
  • Primarily using Chrome (most used web browser)
  • Wants to know how we overcame problems with designs from prior years
  • Wants to know what competitions we're participating in
  • Wants to know how well we've done historically in order to inform how well we'll likely do in the future
  • Robots page should have engineering notes, if available (even if heavily simplified)
  • Robots page should have competition results associated with it
  • Competition links page should make clear and unambiguous what competitions we're participating in
  • Any sections repeating information they would know should be easily skippable (e.g. a section detailing game rules for a game they also designed a robot for)
